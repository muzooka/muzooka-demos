Experiences

 

This shady retreat will feature Southern, locally-inspired craft cocktails in addition to a curated wine bar serving up several varieties. Grab a bite from local and regional eateries dishing out tasty treats, shop handmade crafts, and more. Full details on participating artisans, food offerings, and more can be found here.

Cheers! In-between shows at High Water sit back, relax, and try some of our favorite local libations at The Porch: A Charleston Craft Beer Experience featuring 9 breweries to quench your thirst! Find out more here.

Were kicking off the inaugural High Water weekend with our Low Tide Social on Friday, April 21 at 6pm for an evening of supper and song! Join us for a night complete with live music and one of our favorite Charleston traditions a low country boil hosted by The Smoking Pot. Join us for food and libations followed by special performances from Americana legend Lucinda Williams, folk-rock troubadour Langhorne Slim, and South Carolinas own Ranky Tanky celebrating the best of Southern roots music. Find out more here.

Join us in celebrating the best of Southern cuisine and culture with local chefs collaborating on their favorite dishes for a unique, family-style dining experience at High Water. At Pass the Peas, fans will enjoy locally crafted libations, artfully prepared and hand-selected food, as well as wines paired from around the world. This curated brunch series will have you breaking bread with Charlestons finest in culinary arts. Find out more here.

Seaborn Oyster Co. is bringing High Water Festival attendees four exclusive oyster education classes, teaching you everything youve ever wanted to know about Charlestons favorite bivalve!

The one-hour class, each limited to 10 people, will introduce the basics of oysters and oyster-farming and will provide students with the skills and insight needed to shuck and enjoy oysters on their own. Each guest will shuck and taste a dozen oysters from Seaborn Oyster Co. No prior experience with or knowledge of oysters, oyster-tasting, or oyster-shucking is necessary. Beginners welcome! Find out more here!

Calling all fine wine connoisseurs! Were thrilled to offer guided wine tastings with internationally renowned winemakers Ernst Loosen of Dr. Loosen and Pax Mahle of Wind Gap and Pax Wines throughout the High Water weekend. At Meet the Winemakers presented by Stems & Skins, patrons will enjoy a tasting of world-class wines with the winemaker himself and a full pour of your favorite wine of choice from the event, in addition to a GoVino wine glass to commemorate the experience. Find out more here.

Travel

All roads lead to High Water.

Getting Here

Charlestons location along the Eastern coast makes it a very reasonable drive for many travelers. For those a little further away, the Charleston International Airport (CHS) offers hundreds of flights daily from a wide variety of US cities.

Hotels

We’ve partnered with Hotels for Hope to present our 2017 fan hotel offerings, with a portion from every night booked benefitting our non-profit partners. Secure your room today!

Parking Info

Free parking is available, but limited so festival attendees are encouraged to carpool, rideshare, bike, and arrive early.

Event parking lots can be accessed via Virginia Avenue (preferred access route) or McMillan Avenue. Free shuttles will be available at each designated lot. Bike taxis will be available for a fee.

We also encourage you to ride your bike! We will have a complimentary bike valet service available outside the main entrance.

The rideshare lot can be found by entering SHIPBUILDING WAY into your GPS.
